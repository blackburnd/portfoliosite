1998 AM General H1: A Hands-On History

In 1994, at the Dade County Youth Fair in Miami, I was introduced to the last vehicle I ever thought I'd want. The Forestry Service had an AM General H1 on display, elevated on an articulating frame that let you walk underneath. As a fourteen-year-old who had just spent hours trying to fix my mother's Ford Bronco, I was captivated. My father and I, both amateur mechanics out of necessity, were in awe of its rugged, purpose-built design. He remarked, "You know, this would be the last truck you ever had to buy." He saw a solid foundation where everything else was just a serviceable component—a lesson in durability and smart engineering.

My father never got to ride in an H1 with me, but in 2013, I finally bought one: a 1998 model with 69k miles that a neighbor was letting go. It was my chance to connect with those memories and lessons. This page documents my journey of owning, repairing, and upgrading this incredible machine—a hands-on hobby that has taught me more about mechanical engineering, patience, and problem-solving than I ever imagined.
